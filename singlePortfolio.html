Engineering Journal

Navigating Complexity Through Modular Innovation.

The Nairobi Viaduct presented a unique set of logistical and structural hurdles. Traversing through dense urban corridors required a construction methodology that minimized surface-level disruption while maintaining an aggressive timeline.

Our multi-tier engineering approach utilized a pre-cast segmental launching gantry system. Each segment was cast in a controlled off-site facility and transported during low-traffic windows, allowing the structure to "grow" above the existing city without halting the rhythm of Nairobi's streets.

By implementing a cantilever construction technique, we were able to span existing waterways and utility intersections without the need for temporary ground scaffolding, ensuring that the environmental and infrastructure impact remained negligible.

Innovation Highlights

  • Vibration Isolation

    Advanced elastomeric pads placed between the deck and piers to silence heavy vehicle harmonics.

  • Sustainable Drainage

    Integrated bio-retention basins at off-ramp points to filter runoff before entering the city system.

  • Smart Sensors

    Fiber-optic strain gauges embedded in the structure for real-time integrity monitoring via 5G.
